# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

NYC locals and coffee enthusiasts discovering a new neighborhood café. They arrive curious — either because they live nearby and spotted a "coming soon" sign, or because they care about craft coffee and heard about a place that makes everything from scratch. Their context: quick mobile visit, deciding whether this place is "for them." They want to feel welcomed, not judged — whether they're a taxi driver grabbing a quick latte or a pour-over purist looking for quality. They're evaluating: Is this place approachable? Do they care about what I care about? Will I feel at home here?

## Product Purpose

ka fe is a pre-opening marketing site for a neighborhood café in New York City, launching Spring 2027. The site builds anticipation, communicates what makes the café different (made-from-scratch drinks, personal attention, a short menu done right), and captures emails for launch announcements. Success: visitors feel the warmth and craft before they ever walk in, and the interested ones leave their email.

## Brand Personality

**Personal, craft, warm.**

Voice is confident but never pretentious — the kind of barista who remembers your order, not the kind who judges your choice. Tone is direct and honest: "A short menu, done right" over "curated artisanal offerings." Copy is conversational, not performative.

Emotional goal: make everyone feel welcome and confident. From the taxi driver to the coffee snob, the site should say "this place is for you."

## Anti-references

- **Pretentious third-wave:** No intimidating coffee-snob vibes. No "you're not cool enough" gatekeeping energy. No obscure terminology without explanation.
- **Generic chain café:** Not Starbucks, nothing mass-produced or corporate. No stock photography of people laughing at salads.
- **Overly rustic/farmhouse:** No reclaimed wood clichés, no forced "artisanal" aesthetic. No mason jars, no chalkboard menu fonts.

## Design Principles

1. **Welcome everyone.** Every design choice should make visitors feel like they belong, not like they need to prove themselves.
2. **Show, don't tell.** Don't say "artisanal" — show the spotted bananas slow-cooking to toffee. Let the craft speak through specifics.
3. **Warmth through restraint.** Minimalism that feels inviting, not cold. The paper-warm palette and breathing space create comfort, not austerity.
4. **Personal attention, scaled.** The site should feel like a conversation, not a broadcast. Copy addresses "you" directly.
5. **Confidence without arrogance.** State what you do well simply. No hedging, no hyperbole.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard web accessibility best practices. Good contrast ratios, keyboard navigation, screen reader support, reduced motion alternatives. The goal is a site that works for everyone — aligned with the brand's "welcome everyone" principle.
